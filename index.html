The European Rabbit

The European rabbit is well known for digging networks of burrows, called warrens, where it spends most of its time when not feeding.

  • Scientific name: Oryctolagus cuniculus
  • Habitat: Social animals, living in medium-sized colonies
  • Social organization: Warrens that contain 2 to 10 other individuals
  • Main power: Cuteness

The European rabbit is a smallish, grey-brown (or sometimes black) mammal. Rabbits have long ears, large hind legs, and short, fluffy tails. They move by hopping, using their long and powerful hind legs.
